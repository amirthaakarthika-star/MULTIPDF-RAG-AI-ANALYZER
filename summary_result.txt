The key points of this employment guide are:

1. **Key Employment Terms (KETs)**: Employers must provide KETs in writing to employees hired on or after 1 April 2016, and employed for a continuous period of 14 days or more.
2. **Contents of KETs**: KETs must include details such as:
	* Employment details (e.g. employer's name, employee's name, job title, start date)
	* Working hours and rest days
	* Salary details (e.g. basic salary, allowances, deductions)
	* Leave and medical benefits
	* Other terms (e.g. probation period, notice period)
3. **Form of KETs**: KETs can be provided in soft or hard copy, and common key employment terms can be included in an employee handbook and/or company intranet.
4. **Timing of KETs**: KETs must be issued to employees within 14 days after the first day of employment.
5. **Itemized Pay Slips**: Employers must provide itemized pay slips to employees, which must include details such as:
	* Salary details (e.g. basic salary, overtime pay)
	* Allowances and deductions
	* Overtime details
	* Net salary paid
6. **Consolidated Pay Slips**: Employers can consolidate pay slips if payments are made more than once a month, but the consolidated pay slip must contain details of all payments made since the last pay slip.

Overall, the guide aims to ensure that employees are aware of their key employment terms and are provided with transparent and accurate information about their employment.
